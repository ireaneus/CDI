Session 9 - Ephesians Sermon - Gemini

Date: 2017
Instructor: Robert Dore
Course: Evangelism Teaching and Preaching

Sermon Manuscript: The Architecture of Eternal Security

Text: Ephesians 1:3-14

3 “Blessed be the God and Father of our Lord Jesus Christ, who has blessed us with every spiritual blessing in the heavenly places in Christ,
4 just as He chose us in Him before the foundation of the world, that we would be holy and blameless before Him. In love
5 He predestined us to adoption as sons through Jesus Christ to Himself, according to the kind intention of His will,
6 to the praise of the glory of His grace, which He freely bestowed on us in the Beloved.
7 In Him we have redemption through His blood, the forgiveness of our trespasses, according to the riches of His grace 8 which He lavished on us. In all wisdom and insight
9 He made known to us the mystery of His will, according to His kind intention which He purposed in Him
10 with a view to an administration suitable to the fullness of the times, that is, the summing up of all things in Christ, things in the heavens and things on the earth. In Him
11 also we have obtained an inheritance, having been predestined according to His purpose who works all things after the counsel of His will,
12 to the end that we who were the first to hope in Christ would be to the praise of His glory.
13 In Him, you also, after listening to the message of truth, the gospel of your salvation—having also believed, you were sealed in Him with the Holy Spirit of promise,
14 who is given as a pledge of our inheritance, with a view to the redemption of God’s own possession, to the praise of His glory.”

I. INTRODUCTION

Let’s take a step back in time for a moment and think about our own salvation story. [Pause]

Do you remember when you came to Christ? Many of us describe it like this:

  • “I gave my life to Jesus.”
  • “A friend invited me to church.”
  • “I made a decision to follow Jesus.”
  • “I was searching... trying to figure things out.”

And those things are real. Scripture affirms them in

Romans 10:9: “that if you confess with your mouth Jesus as Lord, and believe in your heart that God raised Him from the dead, you will be saved;”

But Scripture also tells us something deeper.

1 John 4:19 says: “We love, because He first loved us.”

And Jesus Himself says in

John 6:44: “No one can come to Me unless the Father who sent Me draws him…”

[The Tension] We remember the day we "accepted" the gift. We remember the day we "found" Jesus. And for many of us, that feels like Chapter One, Page One of our life with God.

[The Problem Question] But we have to ask ourselves: Is the point at which we "came to Jesus" really our beginning? Was it simply our choosing, or is there a deeper story? What does the Bible say about where our story actually begins? [Pause]

[The Big Idea Answer] The answer we find in Ephesians 1 is that your story didn't start in a church pew or at an altar; it started in the mind of God. Which means what we often call the beginning… is actually our response to something God started long before.

II. THE ROAD MAP (Transitions)

To see where our story truly begins, we are going to look at three stages of God’s sovereign work in Ephesians 1:

  1. The Father’s Plan: We will look at how He chose us before time began (v. 3–6).
  2. The Son’s Provision: We will see how He paid for us in time (v. 7–12).
  3. The Spirit’s Seal: We will see how He secured us for all time (v. 13–14).

Imagine: The Triune God—Father, Son, and Spirit—was there when He created everything. And now, that same Triune God is fully involved in your salvation. [Pause for effect]

Think about the scale of that. This is the Sovereign God who tells the waves exactly how far they can go before they must stop. This is the God who keeps the planets in their orbits and calls the stars by name.

But He isn't just a distant Force. He is an intimate, Personal God. He is the God who knows the exact number of hairs on your head at this very moment. He is the God who watches the sparrow fall and ensures that not one is forgotten.

All of creation exists because of Him, to display "the riches of His grace which He lavished upon us" for the praise of His glory.

III. THE FATHER’S PLAN: ELECTION (v. 3-6)

[Verbal Bridge]: To see where our story truly begins, we have to look past our own birth, past our own decisions, and look into the heart of the Father.

Look at Verse 3:

“Blessed be the God and Father of our Lord Jesus Christ, who has blessed us with every spiritual blessing in the heavenly places in Christ.”

Notice that Paul begins with a shout of praise! One long sentence known as a Berakah a Jewish blessing. He tells us that God has already given us "every spiritual blessing." Every single thing we need for our soul’s security has already been granted in the heavenly places.

But how? And when? Look at Verse 4:

“just as He chose us in Him before the foundation of the world, that we would be holy and blameless before Him.”

Before the foundation of the world. [Pause]

This is the "appointed time" we talked about. This is where your story begins, it starts with God’s "choice" of you.

You were chosen because He is Gracious, and you were adopted to make you a holy and blameless child. As Augustine said,

‘Grace goes before to make us willing, and follows after to keep us willing.’ In other words, God doesn’t just respond to your will—He creates it.

[Verbal Bridge]: But this creates a serious question: If we are chosen to be "holy and blameless," but we know our own hearts are full of sin and "bad choices," how can a Holy God bridge that gap? That brings us to our second point: The Son’s Provision.

IV. THE SON’S PROVISION: REDEMPTION (v. 7-12)

[Verbal Bridge]: If the Father’s Plan was the blueprint drawn before time, the Son’s Provision is the price paid in time to make that plan a reality.

Look at Verse 7:

“In Him we have redemption through His blood, the forgiveness of our trespasses, according to the riches of His grace”

Think about that word: Redemption. [Pause] In the first century, this was "marketplace" language. It meant to pay a price to buy a slave out of bondage and set them free. Paul is telling us that while the Father planned our adoption, the Son purchased it.

But notice the currency. It wasn't gold or silver. It was "through His blood." This is where we see the "riches of His grace" that we mentioned earlier. As the text says in Verse 8, this is grace which He "lavished upon us." God isn't stingy with His mercy. He didn't just give you "enough" to get by; He poured it out over your life like a flood.

Why did He do this? Look at Verse 10:

“with a view to an administration suitable to the fullness of the times, that is, the summing up of all things in Christ, things in the heavens and things on the earth.”

This is the "Appointed Time" in action. Your salvation isn't an accident of history. It is part of the "summing up of all things." When Christ died on that cross, He wasn't just hoping someone would choose Him. He was effectively securing the "unwilling" heart that the Father had already chosen.

As we saw with St. Augustine:

“Grace goes before to make us willing, and follows after to keep us willing.”

The Son’s blood is the "following after." It is the legal guarantee that your "bad choices" are washed away, and your "new will" is bought and paid for.

[Verbal Bridge]: But if the Father planned it, and the Son paid for it, how do we know it will last? How do we know we won't lose it tomorrow? That brings us to our final point: The Spirit’s Seal.

V. THE SPIRIT’S SEAL: SECURITY (v. 13-14)

[Verbal Bridge]: We have seen the Father’s Plan from eternity past. We have seen the Son’s Purchase in history. But the question remains for us in the present: How do I know this belongs to me?

Look at Verse 13:

“In Him, you also, after listening to the message of truth, the gospel of your salvation—having also believed, you were sealed in Him with the Holy Spirit of promise,”

Notice the sequence. You listened, you believed, and you were sealed. In the ancient world, a seal was a mark of ownership and authenticity. When a King put his seal on a document, it meant: "This is mine, and I will defend its contents with my life."

This is the "following after" that Augustine spoke of. > “Grace goes before to make us willing, and follows after to keep us willing.”

The Holy Spirit is that "following after" Grace. He is the one who keeps you willing today. He is the one who keeps you from wandering away forever. But it goes even deeper. Look at Verse 14:

“who is given as a pledge of our inheritance, with a view to the redemption of God’s own possession, to the praise of His glory.”

The word "Pledge" here (arrhabōn) is a legal term. It refers to a down payment or an earnest money deposit. It is God’s personal guarantee that the rest of the transaction—your full, final glorification—is coming.

This is how we reconcile our choice and His plan. Yes, you chose to believe. But that choice was the result of the Father’s plan, paid for by the Son’s blood, and is now permanently protected by the Spirit’s seal. As the text says, you are "God’s own possession." You aren't a robot, but you aren't your own master, either. You are a beloved child, bought at a price, and held in a grip that will never let go.

VI. CONCLUSION: THE "SO WHAT?"

[Verbal Bridge]: We have traveled from eternity past to the sealing of our hearts today. Now, we must ask: So what?

We began with a question: Where can we find a security that is truly unshakable?

The answer is here in the text: Our security is found in the Sovereign God who planned, purchased, and sealed us for His own glory.

What does this mean for your Monday morning?

  1. It means Humility. If God chose you before the world began, you have nothing to boast about. You are a trophy of His grace.
  2. It means Absolute Peace. If your salvation depended on your faithfulness, you should be terrified. But because it depends on HIS faithfulness, you can rest.
  3. It means Purpose. You are not an accident. You are living the exact number of days God predetermined so that you might live "to the praise of His glory" (v. 14).

The Call: If you are hearing this today, perhaps today is the day God has appointed for you to respond. Look away from yourself and look to the blood of Christ.

The Exit: I have preached the words, but I am just a man. I cannot change your heart. I now leave you to the Holy Spirit, who alone provides the conviction and the application to your soul.

Soli Deo Gloria.
