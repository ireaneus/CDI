**Historical Background**

- Isaiah ministered in Jerusalem at the time of the fall of the northern kingdom of Israel in 722 BC.
- The Assyrians conquered Israel and took captive the 10 northern tribes.
- Their intent was to do the same to Judah.
- God destroyed the Assyrian army and rescued Jerusalem in answer to the prayers of Hezekiah and Isaiah.
- It was at this time that Hezekiah was healed and given 15 more years of life.
- Hezekiah‚Äôs son, Manasseh, became king at age 12 and did not have the faith of his father.
- Manasseh‚Äôs wickedness would contribute to the disastrous moral decline of Judah.
- God allowed 100 years of peace in Judah. During that time, Assyria was destroyed by a confederation of northern powers ‚Äì Media, Persia and Babylon.

> *‚ÄúAs her soul was departing (for she died), she named him Ben-oni; but his father called him Benjamin.‚Äù*

## Key Observations

- **Isaiah precedes** Jeremiah but **overlaps historically** in the late Assyrian / early Babylonian transition.
- **Jeremiah and Ezekiel are true contemporaries**, one in Jerusalem, one in exile.
- **Daniel overlaps with both**, serving in Babylon while Jeremiah writes to exiles.
- All four prophets:
    - Proclaim **judgment**
    - Affirm **God‚Äôs sovereignty**
    - Anchor hope in a **future, righteous Kingdom**
- Their messages **do not contradict**, but **layer progressively**, pointing forward to Christ.
