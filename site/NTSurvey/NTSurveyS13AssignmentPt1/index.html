NTSurvey - Session 13 Part 1 - 1 Timothy

Date: A.D. 62
Writer: Paul the Apostle
Category: Pastoral Epistle
Description: Paul wrote to assist Timothy in his oversight of the church, Timothy was reminded of his authority and of his ministry responsibilities. This letter is also an instruction to the church about their responsibilities in the local assembly.
BibleReference: 1 Timothy

CDI Notes - Content of 1 Timothy

Instructors Guide (pg.256)

Author

  • Paul
  • Wrote to Timothy while Timothy was at Ephesus
  • Paul had:
  • Left Timothy at Ephesus
  • Instructed him to remain and deal with doctrinal issues
  • (1 Timothy 1:3)

Historical Setting

  • Written after Paul’s first Roman imprisonment
  • Likely written between A.D. 63–65
  • Paul had resumed missionary travel
  • Churches regularly visited included:
  • Thessalonica
  • Philippi
  • Apollonia
  • Berea

Destination

  • Ephesus
  • Major city of Asia Minor
  • Religious, political, and commercial center
  • Known for:
  • Temple of Artemis (Diana)
  • Pagan worship
  • False teaching influences within the church

Occasion / Reason for Writing

  • False teaching had arisen in the church
  • Timothy was charged with correcting error
  • Problems addressed included:
  • Doctrinal corruption
  • Improper leadership
  • Moral failures
  • Misuse of the Law
  • Elders were appointed early in the church
  • (Acts 14:23)

Purpose

  • To instruct Timothy in:
  • Sound doctrine
  • Church order
  • Leadership qualifications
  • Proper conduct within the church
  • Emphasis on truth lived out in godliness

Major Themes

  • Sound Doctrine
  • Guarding against false teaching

  • Upholding apostolic truth

  • Church Order

  • Proper worship
  • Leadership roles

  • Discipline within the body

  • Godliness

  • Faith expressed through righteous living
  • Personal integrity in leadership

Christological Emphasis

  • Christ as:
  • Mediator between God and man
    • (1 Timothy 2:5)
  • Revealed in glory
    • (1 Timothy 3:16)
  • Comparison:
  • Ephesians 4:3–9
  • 1 Timothy 3:16; 6:15–16
  • Early use of hymns and creedal statements

Key Objections / Challenges Addressed

  • False teachers:
  • Promoted myths and genealogies
  • Misused the Law
  • Emphasis on:
  • Law being used lawfully
  • Grace producing obedience

Outline / Content Overview

  • Greeting

  • (1 Timothy 1:1–2)

  • False Teaching Addressed

  • (1 Timothy 1:3–20)

  • Church Conduct

  • Prayer and worship (2:1–15)

  • Qualifications for overseers and deacons (3:1–13)

  • The Mystery of Godliness

  • (1 Timothy 3:16)

  • Warnings and Instructions

  • Apostasy (4:1–16)
  • Treatment of others (5:1–25)

  • Instruction concerning money (6:3–19)

  • Closing

  • (1 Timothy 6:20ff)

Key Verses

1 Timothy 3:15

but in case I am delayed, I write so that you will know how one ought to conduct himself in the household of God, which is the church of the living God, the pillar and support of the truth.

Theme of 1 Timothy

The emphasis of this letter is the proper ministry and administration of the local church. A variety of subjects are dealt with in order to ensure that the church would be all that it should be.

1 Timothy Summary

In 1 Timothy, Paul writes to his true child in the faith to instruct him in correcting false teaching and establishing proper order within the church at Ephesus (1:3–5). Paul emphasizes sound doctrine, godly leadership, and conduct fitting for the household of God (3:15). He addresses prayer, worship, church leadership, and the danger of apostasy, calling Timothy to guard the truth entrusted to him (6:20).

Christ is presented as the one mediator between God and man, Christ Jesus (2:5), and the mystery of godliness revealed in glory (3:16).

Believers should conduct themselves properly in the household of God (3:15), pursue godliness (4:7–8), and fight the good fight of faith (6:12).

Class Exercise

Read 1 Timothy.

Discussion:

  1. Identify the false teachings addressed in this letter and explain why they were dangerous to the church.
  2. Compare the qualifications for overseers and deacons in 1 Timothy 3 with those found in Titus 1.
  3. Discuss Paul’s emphasis on godliness and how doctrine is meant to shape daily Christian living.

Ask students to explain why Paul refers to the church as “the pillar and support of the truth” (1 Timothy 3:15) and how this responsibility applies to the modern church.
