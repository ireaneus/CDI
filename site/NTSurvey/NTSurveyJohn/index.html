Basic Outline of John: Jesus as God
I: The Introduction vs. 1:1-51
II. The Ministry of Presentation to the Nation vs. 2:1-12:50
III. The Ministry of Instruction to the Disciples vs. 13:1-17:26
IV. The Minstry of Redemption to the World vs. 18:1-19:42
V. The Ministry of Revelation to the Believers vs. 20:1-21:25

I. The Introduction (vs. 1:1-51)

John Chapter 1

In Chapter 1, John introduces Jesus as the eternal Word, who was with God and is God, through whom all things were made. John the Baptist testifies about Jesus (vs. 29), and Jesus calls His first disciples. Christ is presented as “The Word of God” (vs. 1), “the Lamb of God” (vs. 29), “The Son of God” (vs. 34), and “the King of Israel” (vs. 49). But as many as “received Him” (vs. 12), follow Him as His disciples, and declare, “We have found the Messiah” (vs. 41).

II. The Ministry of Presentation to the Nation (vs. 2:1-12:50)

John Chapter 2

In Chapter 2, Jesus performs His first miracle at the wedding in Cana, turning water into wine, and cleanses the temple in Jerusalem. At Cana, He reveals His glory, leading His disciples to believe in Him (vs. 11). Christ is presented as the one who has authority over creation and life, revealing Himself as The Temple of God (vs. 19). Believers should trust in His power to transform lives (vs. 11), and honor God’s holiness in worship.

John Chapter 3

In Chapter 3, Jesus teaches Nicodemus about the necessity of being born again to see the kingdom of God and declares God’s love for the world (vs. 16). John the Baptist also exalts Jesus (vs. 30). Christ is presented as the Son of God who gives eternal life to those who believe in Him (vs. 15-16), and the one who came not to judge the world but to save it (vs. 17). Believers are born again by the Spirit (vs. 5-6), live in the light of truth (vs. 21), and exalt Christ in their lives, letting Him increase while they decrease (vs. 30).

John Chapter 4

In Chapter 4, Jesus speaks with the Samaritan woman at the well, revealing Himself as the Messiah and teaching about true worship (vs. 24). He teaches His disciples about the urgency of spiritual harvest (vs. 35), and many Samaritans believe in Him after hearing the woman’s testimony and meeting Him. Christ is presented as the Living Water (vs. 14), the Messiah (vs. 26), and the Savior of the world (vs. 42). Believers should worship God in spirit and truth (vs. 24), share the gospel boldly (vs. 39), and work in His harvest, rejoicing when others come to know Him (vs. 36).

John Chapter 5

In Chapter 5, Jesus heals a man at the pool of Bethesda, testifies the Father is working, even on the Sabbath, and declares that He and the Father are equal. John lists the testimonies confirming Jesus’ identity: John the Baptist (vs. 33-35), The works of Jesus (vs. 36), The Father Himself (vs. 37), The Scriptures (vs. 39). Christ is presented as equal to the Father and the one sent by the Father to do His work, whose identity is affirmed by these testimonies. Believers should heed Jesus’ words to “Do not sin anymore” (vs. 14), trust the multiple testimonies of who Christ is, and receive eternal life by believing in Him (vs. 24).

John Chapter 6

In Chapter 6, Jesus feeds the five thousand, walks on water, and declares Himself the “bread of life” (vs. 35), saying, “No one can come to Me unless the Father who sent Me draws him” (vs. 44), and “All that the Father gives Me will come to Me, and the one who comes to Me I will certainly not cast out” (vs. 37). His teaching, (vs. 53), offends many and reveals those who truly believe. Christ is presented as not coming to bring peace but a sword, the bread of life given for the salvation of the world (vs. 51), and the one who gives eternal life to all the Father has given Him (vs. 39-40). Believers should cry out to Him, “Lord, to whom shall we go? You have words of eternal life. We have believed and have come to know that You are the Holy One of God” (vs. 68-69).

John Chapter 7

In Chapter 7, Jesus attends the Feast of Booths (Sukkot), a festival commemorating God’s provision during Israel’s wilderness journey and celebrating the ingathering of the harvest. Amidst growing controversy over His identity, (vs. 37), offering the living water that fulfills the spiritual longing symbolized by the feast’s water-drawing rituals. Christ is presented as the source of Living Water and the one sent by the Father, the fulfillment of God’s promises symbolized in the Feast of Booths (vs. 40-43). Believers should come to Jesus for the living water of the Spirit, trusting His words, “The one who believes in Me…from his innermost being will flow rivers of living water” (vs. 38).

John Chapter 8

In Chapter 8, Jesus shows mercy to the woman caught in adultery, (vs. 7). He confronts the Pharisees about their rejection of Him. Jesus emphasizes the freedom that comes from abiding in His word (vs. 32). Christ is presented as the Great I Am (vs. 58) Believers should come to the saving knowledge and put their faith in the Great I AM

John Chapter 9

In Chapter 9, Jesus heals a man born blind (vs. 5). The healing leads to an intense investigation by the Pharisees, who refuse to acknowledge Jesus’ work, revealing their spiritual blindness. The man boldly testifies, “One thing I do know: that though I was blind, now I see” (vs. 25), and believes in Jesus as the Son of Man. Christ is presented as the Light of the world who opens both physical and spiritual eyes (vs. 5, 39). Believers should proclaim Christ’s transforming work in their lives, saying, “Lord, I believe” (vs. 38), and walk in His light, rejecting spiritual blindness.

John Chapter 10

In Chapter 10, Jesus describes Himself as the Good Shepherd, (vs. 11). Christ is presented as the Good Shepherd who knows His sheep, calls them by name, and gives His life for them (vs. 11, 14-15). Believers should listen to His voice, follow Him faithfully, and trust in His eternal security (vs. 27-28).

John Chapter 11

In Chapter 11, Jesus raises Lazarus from the dead, (vs. 25). This miracle demonstrates His power over death and foreshadows His own resurrection, but it also intensifies opposition from the religious leaders, who plot to kill Him. Christ is presented as the Resurrection and the Life, and reveals God’s glory (vs. 25, 40). Believers should declare with Martha, “Yes, Lord; I have come to believe that You are the Christ, the Son of God” (vs. 27).

John Chapter 12

In Chapter 12, Jesus is anointed by Mary at Bethany, enters Jerusalem triumphantly, and speaks about His impending death (vs. 23). He teaches on a grain of wheat, (vs. 24) pointing to His sacrificial death and its redemptive purpose. Christ is presented as the humble King who comes in fulfillment of prophecy (vs. 15), the suffering servant who willingly lays down His life (vs. 27-28), and the light of the world who calls people out of darkness (vs. 46). Believers should follow Him, bearing fruit in their lives, and walk in the light (vs. 36).

III. The Upper Room Discourse (Chapters 13-17)

John Chapter 13

In Chapter 13, Jesus washes His disciples’ feet, demonstrating humble service, and gives them a new commandment to love one another as He has loved them (vs. 34). He predicts Judas’s betrayal and Peter’s denial (vs. 38), and preparing His disciples for what is to come. Christ is presented as the humble servant and loving Master who exemplifies selfless love and service (vs. 14-15). Believers should follow His example of humility, obey His command to love one another, and trust in His faithfulness, even when facing trials and betrayal (vs. 34-35).

John Chapter 14

In Chapter 14, Jesus comforts His disciples, assures them He is preparing a place for them in the Father’s house. He promises the coming of the Holy Spirit, who will teach and remind them of His words. Christ is presented as the way to the Father, the truth that reveals God, and the life that gives eternal hope (vs. 6). Believers should trust in Him, keep His commandments (vs. 15), and find peace in His promise: “Peace I leave you; My peace I give you” (vs. 27).

John Chapter 15

In Chapter 15, Jesus teaches about abiding in Him,(vs. 5). He commands His disciples to love one another as He has loved them, and warns them that the world will hate them because they are not of the world. Christ is presented as the true vine, the source of spiritual life and fruitfulness for all who abide in Him (vs. 1-5). Believers should remain in His love, obey His command to love one another (vs. 12), and stand firm in their faith despite the world’s opposition (vs. 18-19).

John Chapter 16

In Chapter 16, Jesus prepares His disciples for His departure, promising the Holy Spirit, who will guide them into all truth and convict the world concerning sin, righteousness, and judgment (vs. 8). He foretells their sorrow turning to joy(vs. 20), and assures them of peace in Him. Christ is presented as the one who overcomes the world, giving His followers peace and victory amid tribulation (vs. 33). Believers should take heart in His victory, rely on the Spirit’s guidance (vs. 13), and pray in His name with confidence (vs. 24).

John Chapter 17

In Chapter 17, Jesus prays for His glorification, His disciples, and all future believers, asking the Father to sanctify them in truth and to make them one as He and the Father are one (vs. 3). Christ is presented as the great High Priest who intercedes for His followers and ensures their unity and sanctification (vs. 17-19). Believers should grow in knowledge of God and Jesus, live in unity with fellow believers, and be sanctified through the truth (vs. 17).

IV. The Passion and Resurrection of the Savior (Chapters 18-21)

John Chapter 18

In Chapter 18, Jesus is betrayed by Judas, arrested, and brought before Annas, Caiaphas, and Pilate. Jesus declares His kingdom is not of this world (vs. 37). Christ is presented as the True King and submits willingly to His path of suffering (vs. 36-37). Believers should remain faithful to Him even in adversity and testify to His truth in a world opposed to His kingdom (vs. 37).

John Chapter 19

In Chapter 19, Jesus is condemned, crucified, and buried. He fulfills Scripture, saying, “It is finished” (vs. 30), and offers His life as the Lamb of God to take away the sin of the world. Christ is presented as the crucified Savior who completes His mission to redeem humanity (vs. 30). Believers should behold the cross with reverence, trust in the sufficiency of His sacrifice, and live in the freedom it provides (vs. 28-30).

John Chapter 20

In Chapter 20, Jesus rises from the dead, appears to Mary Magdalene, and commissions His disciples, (vs. 21). He invites Thomas to believe, saying, “Blessed are those who did not see, and yet believed” (vs. 29). Christ is presented as the risen Lord who brings peace and life to all who believe in Him (vs. 31). Believers should proclaim His resurrection boldly, walk in the peace He gives, and trust Him even when they cannot see (vs. 29).

John Chapter 21

In Chapter 21, Jesus appears to His disciples by the Sea of Tiberias, provides a miraculous catch of fish, and restores Peter (vs. 15-17). He commissions Peter to “tend My sheep” and speaks of his future faithfulness and martyrdom. Christ is presented as the risen Shepherd who restores and commissions His followers to care for His flock (vs. 15-17). Believers should love Christ deeply, follow Him wholeheartedly, and fulfill their calling to serve His people (vs. 19).
