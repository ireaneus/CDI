Paul's Second letter to the Corinthians
I. Introduction (vs. 1:1-11)
II. Paul's Personal Testimony (vs. 1:12-2:13)
III. Paul's Teachings about the True Ministry of Christ (vs. 2:14-6:10)
IV. Paul's Exhortations to the Corinthians (vs. 6:11-7:16)
V. Paul's Instructions About Giving (vs. 8:1-9:15)
VI. Paul's Defense of His Apostolic Ministry (vs. 10:1-13:10)
VII. Conclusion (vs. 13:11-14)

I. Introduction vs.1:1-11

2 Corinthians Chapter 1

In Chapter 1, Paul praises God as the source of comfort, explains how God comforts believers so they can comfort others, and shares his experience of severe suffering in Asia, which led him to rely on God (vv. 3–10). He clarifies his change in travel plans and affirms his sincerity toward the Corinthians (vv. 12–17). Paul assures them that God’s promises are fulfilled in Christ and that believers are established and sealed by God with the Holy Spirit as a guarantee (vv. 18–22). Christ is presented as the fulfillment of all God’s promises (v. 20), Believers should rely on God, comfort others, and walk in sincerity (vv. 4, 9, 12).

II. Paul's Personal Testimony vs.1:12-2:13

2 Corinthians Chapter 2

In Chapter 2, Paul urges the Corinthians to forgive and comfort a repentant sinner, explains his concern during his journey to Troas and Macedonia, and thanks God for leading believers in triumph in Christ (vv. 5–17). Christ is presented as the triumphant leader who spreads the fragrance of His knowledge through believers (v. 14), Believers should forgive those who repent, comfort the sorrowful, and live as the aroma of Christ, aroma from death to death, to the other an aroma from life to life (vv. 7–10, 15–16).

III. Paul's Teachings about the True Ministry of Christ vs.2:14-6:10

2 Corinthians Chapter 3

In Chapter 3, Paul contrasts the fading glory of the old covenant with the lasting glory of the new covenant, where the Spirit gives life and believers are transformed into Christ’s image (vv. 6–18). Christ is presented as the Glory of God revealed as the veil is removed (vv. 14, 18), Believers should live by the Spirit and reflect God’s glory (vv. 6, 18).

2 Corinthians Chapter 4

In Chapter 4, Paul speaks of enduring hardships by God’s mercy, proclaiming the gospel despite their frailty, and trusting in God’s power. He encourages believers to focus on eternal unseen things, not temporary afflictions (vv. 1–18). Christ is presented as the light of God’s glory and the source of eternal life through His resurrection (vv. 6, 14), Believers should persevere through trials, trust in God’s power, and focus on eternal realities (vv. 8–9, 16–18).

2 Corinthians Chapter 5

In Chapter 5, Paul speaks of the believer’s hope in an eternal body, walking by faith, and the ministry of reconciliation through Christ (vv. 1–20). Christ is presented as the one who knew no sin to become sin on our behalf (v. 21), Believers should walk by faith, aim to please God, and serve as ambassadors of reconciliation (vv. 7, 9, 20).

2 Corinthians Chapter 6

In Chapter 6, Paul urges believers not to receive God’s grace in vain, highlights his endurance in ministry, and calls them to be separate from unbelievers, emphasizing that they are the temple of the living God (vv. 1–18). Christ is presented as their God, the Lord Almighty (vv. 16–18), Believers should come out from their midst, be separate, and avoid what is unclean (vv. 17–18).

IV. Paul's Exhortations to the Corinthians vs. 6:11-7:16

2 Corinthians Chapter 7

In Chapter 7, Paul expresses joy over the Corinthians' repentance, recalling how his previous letter caused sorrow that led to genuine repentance and salvation (vv. 8–10). He commends them for their earnestness in clearing themselves and for their godly grief, which produced a lasting change (vv. 11–12). Paul rejoices in their comfort and obedience, noting how his associate Titus was also encouraged by them (vv. 13–16). Christ is presented as the one who brings comfort and joy through repentance and reconciliation (vv. 6, 9), Believers should pursue godly repentance that leads to salvation and live in obedience and sincerity (vv. 9–11, 15).

V. Paul's Instructions About Giving vs. 8:1-9:15

2 Corinthians Chapter 8

In Chapter 8, Paul urges the Corinthians to complete their collection for the saints, using the Macedonians as an example of generosity, and reminds them that giving should be voluntary and based on what they have (vv. 1–24). Christ is presented as the one who, though rich, became poor so that through His poverty believers might become rich (v. 9), Believers should give generously, willingly, and according to their ability (vv. 7, 12–14).

2 Corinthians Chapter 9

In Chapter 9, Paul urges the Corinthians to give generously, reminding them that those who give abundantly will be blessed, and that giving should be cheerful and voluntary (vv. 1–12). Christ is presented as the one through whom God provides abundantly for every good work (vv. 8–10), Believers should give generously, cheerfully, and trust God to supply their needs (vv. 6–7, 10–11).

VI. Paul's Defense of His Apostolic Ministry vs.10:1-13:10

2 Corinthians Chapter 10

In Chapter 10, Paul defends his apostolic authority, explaining that his ministry is conducted with divine power to demolish strongholds and take every thought captive to obey Christ (vv. 1–6). He contrasts his humble presence with his bold letters, urging the Corinthians not to judge by appearances but to recognize his God-given authority (vv. 7–18). Christ is presented as the source of divine power for spiritual warfare and obedience (vv. 4–5), Believers should trust in God’s power, reject worldly judgments, and take every thought captive to obey Christ (vv. 5, 7, 17).

2 Corinthians Chapter 11

In Chapter 11, Paul warns the Corinthians against being deceived by false apostles and presents himself as a faithful servant of Christ, enduring hardships for the gospel (vv. 1–15). He recounts his sufferings, including imprisonments, beatings, shipwrecks, and dangers faced during his ministry (vv. 23–33). Christ is presented as the true Lord whom Paul faithfully serves despite great suffering (vv. 2–4), Believers should discern truth from falsehood and remain devoted to Christ (vv. 3–4, 13–15).

2 Corinthians Chapter 12

In Chapter 12, Paul speaks of a vision of heaven he received and the thorn in his flesh given to keep him humble. He emphasizes that God’s grace is sufficient and His power is perfected in weakness (vv. 1–10). Paul defends his ministry again, reminding the Corinthians of the signs of an apostle he performed among them and expressing his concern for their spiritual well-being (vv. 11–21). Christ is presented as the one whose grace is sufficient and whose power is made perfect in weakness (vv. 9–10), Believers should boast in their weaknesses and rely on Christ’s grace and power (vv. 9–10, 19).

2 Corinthians Chapter 13

In Chapter 13, Paul calls the Corinthians to examine themselves to see if they are in the faith and warns them of his potential disciplinary visit if they do not repent (vv. 1–6). He prays for their restoration and urges them to aim for full maturity and peace (vv. 7–11). Paul concludes with a final blessing, calling on the grace of Christ, the love of God, and the fellowship of the Holy Spirit to be with them (vv. 11–14). Christ is presented as the source of grace for believers (v. 14), Believers should examine themselves, aim for restoration, and live in peace and unity (vv. 5, 9, 11).
