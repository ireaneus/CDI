Session 5 Part 1 - The Enternal Decrees of God

Concept 1

From eternity, God has had an unchangeable plan with regard to His creation.

Notes - Absolute Sovereign - Omnipotent

Scriptures - Ephesians 1:4

just as He chose us in Him before the foundation of the world, that we would be holy and blameless before Him. In love- 2 Timothy 1:9 - 2 Timothy 1:9 who has saved us and called us with a holy calling, not according to our works, but according to His own purpose and grace which was granted us in Christ Jesus from all eternity

Human Free Agency

Notes

Anthropocentric and Theocentric are two terms that describe different perspectives in theology and philosophy: - Anthropocentric (from anthropos, meaning "human") refers to a human-centered viewpoint. In theology, it emphasizes humanity's role, needs, and experiences as central. It often focuses on how religious beliefs or practices benefit people or how God relates specifically to human life. - Theocentric (from theos, meaning "God") refers to a God-centered viewpoint. This perspective prioritizes God as the central focus of everything, emphasizing His will, glory, and purposes as supreme. In theology, this viewpoint emphasizes God’s sovereignty and the idea that all creation, including humanity, exists to glorify Him.

In summary, anthropocentric focuses on humanity, while theocentric focuses on God.
